Scientific Overview

Oceanography is the multidisciplinary study of the global ocean. It integrates various sciences to understand the pulse of our planet, covering 71% of Earth's surface.

Marine Biology

Marine biology explores the life forms that inhabit the sea. Life is organized into vertical zones based on light penetration.

Physical Oceanography

This branch focuses on the physical properties of the ocean, including currents, waves, and tides.

Chemical Oceanography

Chemical oceanographers study the salinity and chemical composition of seawater and how the ocean acts as a "carbon sink."

Marine Geology

Marine geologists investigate underwater volcanoes, hydrothermal vents, and tectonic plate boundaries.

Ocean Circulation

The Global Conveyor Belt is a massive system of deep-ocean circulation driven by temperature and salinity.

Ocean Acidification

As the ocean absorbs more CO2, the water becomes more acidic, threatening coral reefs and shell-building organisms.
