Scientific Overview

Oceanography is an interdisciplinary science integrating biology, chemistry, physics, and geology. Covering 71% of the Earth's surface, the ocean regulates our climate and supports half of all life on Earth.

Marine Biology & Ecology

Marine biology explores the life forms that inhabit the sea, from microscopic phytoplankton to the midnight abyss.

Physical Oceanography

This branch analyzes the mechanics of waves, tides, and massive currents like the Gulf Stream.

Chemical Oceanography

Examining the "chemical signature" of seawater, including salinity and dissolved gases.

Marine Geology

Marine geologists explore the seafloor's topography, studying seafloor spreading and tectonic movements.
